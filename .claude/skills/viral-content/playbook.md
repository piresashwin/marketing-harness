# Viral B2B Content — Evidence Base & Playbook

Research-backed reference behind the `viral-content` skill. Sources span 2024–2026:
Richard van der Blom's *Algorithm Insights* (1.5M+ posts), Buffer (52M+ posts),
AuthoredUp, Gong Labs, Ordinal's link study, Justin Welsh, and the recruiting-niche
landscape. **Multipliers are directional** — sources conflict on exact magnitudes but
agree on direction; treat single numbers as ranges.

> **Macro-shift framing it all:** LinkedIn views are down ~50% YoY; the algorithm
> pivoted from raw viral reach to "Depth & Authority." ML/NLP classifiers now actively
> *suppress* manipulation (bait, pods, reposts, watermarks, link-pulls) and *reward*
> original, native, human, value-first content with high dwell. The cheap-engagement
> era is closing — proprietary substance and founder voice win.

## A. The recruiting-niche landscape (who Hiredesq is up against / among)

### Direct & adjacent vendors
- **Recruit CRM** — the most content-forward agency-ATS: "Top 50 Recruitment Influencers"
  franchise + recruiter memes. Community-flattery + humor, not features. The one real meme play.
- **Recruiterflow** — heavy SEO/listicle engine for agency owners; low personality.
- **Bullhorn** — the incumbent *everyone positions against*: complex, $99+/user/mo, $3k–$10k
  implementation. 45% of solo recruiters who leave cite complexity/cost. The market's villain.
- **Solo/small-agency challengers (closest positioning rivals):** Happlicant ("best ATS for
  solo recruiters / Bullhorn alternative"), Leonar, Spott (YC W25), Pin. They claim the
  solo lane via comparison pages — not via personality.
- **Sourcing/AI tools (enterprise-leaning, own "AI sourcing" vocab):** Gem, hireEZ, SeekOut,
  Fetcher, Findem, Juicebox. **Employer ATS (not agency):** Ashby, Workable, Teamtailor, Greenhouse.

### Influencers & communities — the trust-laundering distribution nodes
- **Hung Lee / Recruiting Brainfood** — the gravitational center: 41k+ subscriber newsletter,
  active LinkedIn, Brainfood Live. *The* channel/partner to court.
- **Greg Savage / "The Savage Truth"** — patron saint of agency billers; opinionated
  recruiter-craft hot-takes that reliably pull comments. Exactly Hiredesq's audience.
- **Chad & Cheese Podcast** — "HR's most dangerous podcast"; snark + *roasts vendor marketing*.
  The skeptic core — tone reference for what cuts through, and where bad marketing gets mocked.
- **Sourcing/TA voices (more corporate-TA):** Mike "Batman" Cohen, Jan Tegze, Glen Cathey,
  Irina Shamaeva, Stacy Donovan Zapar, William Tincup/RecruitingDaily, Josh Bersin (analyst altitude).
- **Rich Gibbard** (~28k) — closest creator to Hiredesq's buyer-utility niche (free templates for staffing firms).
- **"RecTok"** — a *format* (Day-in-the-Life + corporate humor), not a single account.

### What the recruiting audience engages with
- **Pain (highest relatability):** candidate ghosting (#1 evergreen meme), client/req chaos
  ("client plays recruiter, you do the work"), irrelevant applications, admin grind / tool
  resentment ("updating the ATS instead of recruiting"), and the **WhatsApp/spreadsheet-as-CRM**
  reality (WhatsApp = "the new job board," 500M+ Indian users) — Hiredesq's exact origin story,
  almost nobody makes content about it.
- **Wins:** placements & billings (fees ~20–30% of salary, ~$15k–$25k each). Few share revenue
  transparently → build-in-public billings is wide open.
- **Heated debates:** AI replacing vs. freeing recruiters, recruiters ghosting candidates, "is
  the ATS or the recruiter the problem?", agency vs in-house, fee justification.
- **Tone that cuts through:** founder-led not brand-led; teach/relate don't pitch; humor +
  relatability; "tools are broken / anti-bloat"; opinionated hot-takes over neutral tips.

### Owned white-space (each maps to a Hiredesq differentiator)
1. **Revenue visibility** — uncontested emotional centerpiece.
2. **WhatsApp/spreadsheet chaos → clean DB** — the mess as hero; demoable transformation video.
3. **Solo-recruiter underdog *with personality*** — felt, not just claimed.
4. **Fair/free pricing as narrative** — post the pricing logic; high trust.
5. **Satisfying before/after transformation video** for Reels/RecTok (demo = entertainment).
6. **Court the nodes** (Brainfood feature, Chad & Cheese mention, Savage's audience) over paid reach.

## B. Hook formulas (copy-paste; fill the brackets, then add real specifics)

Rules: first line ≈95% of the work (only lines 1–3 show pre-"see more"); end on an open
loop (colon/question — Zeigarnik effect); hide the "how."

1. **Contrarian / unpopular** *(negativity bias; negative framing can outperform ~59%)*
   - `Unpopular opinion: [contrarian claim about recruiting].`
   - `Everyone tells you to [chase more candidates]. They're wrong. Here's why:`
2. **"I did X for N days — here's what happened"** *(time-box + curiosity gap)*
   - `I [did X] for [N] days. Here's what happened:`
3. **Number + outcome (+ "with no Y" twist)** *(specificity = credibility)*
   - `We grew to $[X]/mo in [N] months with no [funding / sales team / ads].`
4. **Counterintuitive subtraction** *(violated expectation)*
   - `We deleted [our biggest feature]. On purpose. Here's why:`
5. **Mistake / confession** *(authenticity + pattern interrupt)*
   - `I wasted $[X] on [thing] so you don't have to.`
6. **Callout / curiosity gap** *(WIIFM filter)*
   - `If you're a [role], you need to hear this.` / `Nobody tells you this about [topic].`
7. **Listicle** *(odd numbers convert ~20% better — 3/5/7)*
   - `[#] [things] to [outcome] without [conventional method].`
8. **Story open** *(narrative transportation)*
   - `In [year] I was [low point]. Today [transformed state]. Here's what changed:`

**Welsh's signature structure:** Relatable Enemy → Hero → Gasoline + Teaser.
**Acosta's SLAY:** Hook → Re-hook → Lead → Body → Power-ending → CTA.

### Recruiting-tuned ready examples
- "Everyone says chase more candidates. Wrong — your problem is your database, not your pipeline."
- "I ran my whole desk out of WhatsApp for 30 days. Here's exactly what broke:"
- "I wasted $12k on a recruiting CRM we used twice. Here's what I'd buy instead:"
- "Most recruiters can't tell me what they billed last quarter in under a minute. That's a tooling failure."
- "We made the candidate database free forever. Here's how we still make money:"
- "I dragged 200 resumes into one box. Here's what came out 2 minutes later:"

## C. Platform mechanics with numbers

### LinkedIn
- **Structure:** Trailer (above "see more") → Meat → CTA + a visual. Hook = first ~140–210 chars.
- **Reading level:** ~4th–6th grade; above 10th grade loses 35%+ reach. Broetry line breaks +25%; closing question +20–40%.
- **Length:** text-only 600–1,200 chars (sweet spot ~800–1,000); text+image 900–1,500 (−10% reach per +300 over); link posts ≤150.
- **Format reach multipliers (vs baseline):** polls **1.64x** · documents/carousels **1.45x** (≈29% save rate) · images **1.18x** · video **1.10x** · plain text **0.88x**.
- **Carousels:** 1080×1350 (4:5), 5–15 slides (drop-off after 10), <3MB, 30pt+ font, <50 words/slide.
- **Native video:** 30–90s; hook in 2–3s; vertical 9:16; burn-in captions (~35% muted); ~1/wk.
- **Cadence/timing:** 3–5/wk, ≥24h apart, never >1/day. Tue–Thu 8–11am (Wed strongest); 5–10pm now also performs.

### Instagram
- **Reels:** hook in first **1.5s** with motion; ≥60% 3-sec hold target; hook text in frame 1 (+50% retention); completion peaks 15–25s (max length 3 min since Jan 2025); ~85% muted → on-screen captions mandatory (+12% watch, +40% saves), kept in central title-safe zone; trending audio = discovery not retention.
- **Carousels (highest IG engagement ~1.92%):** 8–10 slides; slide 1 hook 5–8 words readable <2s; ~3x saves of single image; non-swipers re-served.
- **Cadence:** 3–5/wk (2x+ follower growth vs 1–2/wk); B2B best 9am–12pm.

### Facebook
- Weak organic Page reach → Groups-first; Reels get a free reach boost; install Meta Pixel for retargeting; repurpose IG assets with slightly more explanatory captions.

## D. Viral mechanics (why content travels)

- **Signal hierarchy:** Comments > Saves > Shares-with-commentary > Reposts > Likes. Comments ≈2x a like; thoughtful (35–120 words) count; reply-threads lift reach up to 2.4x. Saves ≈5x a like's reach. Likes weakest.
- **Golden hour:** algo tests on 2–5% of network in first ~60–120 min, measuring engagement *velocity*; strong early engagement → ~2.3x total reach. **Author replying for ~90 min post-publish is the strongest lever** (within 15 min ≈ +90% boost). Posts now live 2–3 weeks in feed.
- **Native > links:** link-free posts up to 6x reach, ~3x comments, 2.5x dwell. Articles/Newsletters carry no link penalty (reader stays on-platform).
- **Hook → retention → payoff:** <2s to hook (+~30% retention). Line 1 ≤8 words; line 2 ≤12 advertising the payoff. Promise must match payoff.
- **Highest-ceiling formats for a SaaS like Hiredesq:** data-as-content (Gong Labs: analyzed 1M+ calls → 46:54 talk-listen) and build-in-public metrics (RB2B: 0→$5M ARR, 13 mo, 5 people, posted churn/revenue). Both require *proprietary numbers to reveal* — clean-DB transformations, placement counts, revenue.
- **Controversy calibration:** specific, slightly provocative, anchored in a defendable truth (~10% pushback = calibrated; 0% bland; 50% alienates ICP). *(Folk-wisdom, not a citable law.)*

## E. Anti-patterns (kill-the-draft list) + fix

1. External link in body → ≈40–50% fewer impressions; first-comment trick now largely dead (comment links suppressed ~80%). *Fix:* full value native; "comment X, I'll DM it" or edit link in within first minutes.
2. Corporate jargon / "thrilled to announce" → low-signal, raises reading level. *Fix:* first-person, 4th–6th grade, personal profile.
3. Over-pitching. *Fix:* ~4–6 give posts per ask; default call-to-conversation.
4. Stock photos / non-native visuals → down-ranked 30–50%. *Fix:* real photos, screenshots, founder selfies, native export.
5. No hook / buried lede. *Fix:* lead with payoff/contrarian/number.
6. Hashtag overuse → LinkedIn 3 max, IG 3–5. *Fix:* 3–5 relevant + keywords in caption.
7. Engagement-bait ("Comment YES", "Tag a friend") → NLP-suppressed. *Fix:* ask a real expertise question.
8. Frequency errors: >1 LinkedIn/24h cannibalizes; IG delete-and-repost (10+/30 days) excludes from recommendations. *Fix:* 3–5/wk, don't delete-repost.
9. Tagging spam (>5) & **pods** (~97% detection, shadowbans). *Fix:* tag ≤5 relevant; daily genuine niche engagement instead of pods.
10. Walls of text. *Fix:* one idea per line, read on a phone first.
11. Editing after golden hour starts → re-triggers evaluation. *Fix:* proofread before publishing.
12. **Fully AI-generated, voice-flat content** → reported −30% reach / −55% engagement (synthetic-tone + semantic-duplication detection). *Fix:* vary voice, add genuine specifics, never ship a template verbatim.
